Pregnancy Termination Research

User Experience
User Interface

overview

Respectful Pregnancy Termination project was about designing and developing a responsive website for different associations and social movements around mourning due to termination of pregnancy, stillbirth and neonatal death in Spain. Their goal is to run an exploratory research about how women and their families have been supported by the national healthcare system when the pregnancy ends with an interruption. They asked me to design a website to help raise awareness of the survey and subsequently its results.

team

I was the only designer in this project. However, I was in constant contact with several members of the organisation for guidance on the subject.

The main problem to be solved was to facilitate access to surveys for a research on the process of pregnancy interruption in Spain. I needed to come up with some solutions such as adding easy access to the survey on the homepage or show details about the research in a way that it is easy to understand and find for users.

This was a rare opportunity that I enjoyed because I had to do start from scratch. It meant I had to work on everything from the Information architecture and usability to the user interface and responsivity.

understanding

The user experience research helped me understand what sections were necessary on a study page like this, not only to collect data but also to engage with users and provide them with a good experience. After the competence analysis, some interviews with users and the empathy map, it was clear to me which features the site should have.

Problem

The user experience research helped me understand what sections were necessary on a study page like this, not only to collect data but also to engage with users and provide them with a good experience. After the competence analysis, some interviews with users and the empathy map, it was clear to me which features the site should have.

Goals

  • Easy to understand and navigate
  • Objectives of the research clearly expressed
  • Transparency about how data is collected

Success

  • Easy access to the surveys
  • As many participants as possible answering the surveys

Target audience

The research is aimed at two specific groups:
1.

Families who have had the experience of pregnancy termination at any time and for any reason.

2.

Health professionals in the field of public health who work in the area of ​​sexual and reproductive health care.

Findings

The incentive that motivates users to participate in this research is their willingness to drive a change that improves the situation of women who go through an interruption of pregnancy in Spain.

The design has to be responsive and mobile-first since most users will access the surveys through their phones.

The interaction with the page must be visual, with easy and quick access to the surveys.

The elements of the design have to be consistent with each other and have white space so that the page breathes.

The different sections must have subsections as shown in the affinity maps.

The text has to be simple and use plain language. The colours have to be clear, preferably in warm tones.

As for the feeling, this page would have to convey tranquillity and calmness, while being a welcoming page for users who browse.

The website must be well structured, the following sections being necessary:

  • Landing page
  • Resources
  • Contact
  • Research
  • Testimonials

User’s needs

  • Improve the families' care during the process of termination of pregnancy
  • Access information and resources on termination of pregnancy
  • Understand the objective of this study and its consequences

Possible solutions

  • Landing page with clear actions and good navigation
  • Outstanding information hierarchy (AI)
  • Clear CTA buttons to access the surveys
  • FAQ and testimonial pages
  • Easy to share

Exploring

At this point, the first thing we did was to create the content to have the best information architecture we could to answer the user’s needs.

I had to design a website from the beginning so we had some meetings to discuss the content according to previous research results. Then we ideated a web map and decided that the most important flow would be to have clear access to the surveys and a distinct description of them.

Issues

The usability tests tell us that we achieved the main goal and the user can access the surveys effortless and quickly. However, we found some issues that we can fix easily.

  • Users don’t know where to find details about the study
  • Contact info missing on the about page
  • Some changes needed with the questions on the FAQ page
  • Don’t use navigation arrows

Improvements

  • Details about the study.Reduce the secondary navigation bar and move all the study’s information to the same page with subtitles
  • FAQ. Move the current questions to a section on the study’s information page and write other suitable questions to the FAQ section
  • Contact. Add an email on the “about” page
  • Arrows. Delete them

implementation

After iterating on the design, I built the website with HTML, CSS, and JS. The website is still operational.

What I learned

When I began this project, I had no idea what to expect. I loved having the freedom to experiment with different layouts and trying new ways to make the site more accessible and engaging.

I decided to spend the first couple of weeks researching different types of websites in the same field and the kinds of features they included. I was able to learn a lot about the different ways people interact with such websites and how they organize the content.
I wanted it to be easy to navigate and engaging so I made sure to include a variety of graphic elements. I also included a call-to-action button on each of the pages so that users could easily access the surveys and get in touch with the organisation to share information about their experiences. The final result was something I was really happy with!

On the other hand, I would have spent some time on the visual aspect of the surveys. However, the organization decided to use Google forms to collect the data and populate the surveys, which prevented me from being able to create a design that matched the look and feel of the brand. Looking back, I think this is something I would change in the future as I believe it would have made the survey more visually appealing and easier to complete. I would also consider having some accessibility expertise to make sure that the final site was as accessible as possible.

I started with a workshop with the project representatives. We were looking for colours, typography, iconography and imagery. The goal was that they were consistent with the users’ needs and with the feeling that the organisers wanted to convey.

As typography I chose to use Inter. Firstly because it feels clean and has good readbility, and secondly because the wide scope of weights and styles helped with the hierarchy of the page. In addition, this typeface conveys the seriousness and transparency that the project requires.
